THE CALENDAR

SIX WAYS TO RACE

Every race type is a build test, not a lap count. The map tells you what it favours before you commit — then your deck sits the exam. Between races the road forks past pit shops, mystery stops and the Mechanic's upgrade bench.

  • SPRINT

    Short and sharp. Favours boost + accel.

  • GRAND PRIX

    An honest race. Favours nobody.

  • ENDURANCE

    The long haul. Favours engine, scrap + sponsor builds.

  • TRAFFIC JAM

    A packed field. Favours ram, shield + ghost.

  • TECHNICAL

    Hairpin country. Favours brake + handling.

  • DUEL

    One rival. No hiding. Winner takes a souvenir.

THE INSPIRATION SHELF

WHERE THIS GAME COMES FROM

A roguelike deckbuilder, a racing game, and a board-game brain in one toy box. If any of these are your thing, you're in the right paddock.

HEAT: PEDAL TO THE METAL
The board game that lit the fuse. Heat's genius — racing as a hand of cards, push your luck or cook your engine — is the itch this game scratches in real time. Not a licensed adaptation; a video-game-native answer to the same craving. (No affiliation — just admiration. The full story →)
SLAY THE SPIRE
The roguelike deckbuilder blueprint: short runs, a deck that is your character, and one more draft that ruins your bedtime.
SLOT CARS & MICRO MACHINES
Scalextric afternoons, top-down toy circuits, cars the size of a thumb with grudges the size of a bus.
ONE-PERSON GARAGE
An indie game in the honest sense: one developer, an AI pit crew, and a public dev diary of every step — shipped as a free playtest while it grows.
